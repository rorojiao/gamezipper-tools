================================================================
METHODOLOGY
================================================================
1. Pulled current trending puzzle titles from Poki and CrazyGames (Poki 150+ unique, CrazyGames 60+).
2. Cross-checked each candidate against the 267-game catalog supplied in the task brief; flagged any name overlap and removed it.
3. For gap categories the brief asked us to focus on (physics drawing, stacking/tetris variants, number puzzles not yet covered, Nikoli logic puzzles not yet covered, escape room, pattern/sequence, spatial reasoning), enumerated all candidates with positive evidence of demand.
4. Verified Nikoli coverage by reading the full list of Nikoli-published puzzle types on Wikipedia and the official nikoli.co.jp "Puzzles" page - about 25+ Nikoli genres are absent from the GameZipper catalog (Tapa, Yajilin, Nurikabe, Fillomino, Heyawake, Masyu, Shakashaka, Shikaku, Slant, Suguru, Ripple Effect, LITS, Kuromasu, etc.).
5. Scored every candidate 0-5 on each of the five criteria. Cutoff: >=18/25.
6. Ranked finalists; picked TOP 5 by total score, breaking ties by category coverage and market validation (Switch release, Poki/CrazyGames placement, search volume).

================================================================
SCORING RUBRIC
================================================================
  Popularity (0-5)         = downloads, Poki/CrazyGames placement, search volume, Switch/mobile ports
  Browser feasibility (0-5) = single HTML5/Canvas, no server, no Unity
  Uniqueness (0-5)          = gap relative to existing 267-game catalog (5 = not present at all)
  Monetization (0-5)        = session length, ad-break friendliness, casual appeal
  Dev complexity (0-5)      = LOWER is BETTER (5 = trivial, 0 = >60 KB or impossible)
  TOTAL                    = sum / 25. Cutoff: >=18.

----------------------------------------------------------------
#1  NURIKABE
----------------------------------------------------------------
Category:  Nikoli logic puzzle  (gap: "islands in the stream")
Scores:     P 4  B 5  U 5  M 3  D 4   ->  21 / 25
Slug:       nurikabe

Why it wins:
  * Verified demand. Nintendo eShop has an official "Puzzle by Nikoli
    S Nurikabe" Switch title (released 2024, dedicated to this single
    puzzle). Yajilin and Tapa got the same treatment - the trio is the
    "second-tier" of Nikoli, just behind Sudoku/Kakuro in popularity.
  * Distinct from anything in the catalog. Closest neighbors are
    hitori, hashiwokakero, akari, and slitherlink - but Nurikabe's
    "islands" mechanic is genuinely different.
  * Clean Canvas implementation. Grid + per-cell black/white state +
    number clues + 3 rules to validate. BFS for connectivity, simple
    2x2 sweep for the "no 2x2 black" rule. A complete solver fits in
    < 5 KB of minified JS, leaving 50+ KB for the puzzle database
    and UI.
  * Pre-loaded puzzle set. Generate 50-80 hand-curated 8x8 to 14x14
    puzzles inline (or ship a tiny built-in generator using the
    solver in reverse). Stays under 60 KB.
  * Long sessions. Average Nikoli solve is 5-25 minutes - strong
    ad inventory and great D1 retention.

P 4  Two Switch games + steady puzzle-book presence, but still niche vs. sudoku
B 5  Pure grid + clicks; smallest possible asset footprint
U 5  Zero catalog overlap
M 3  Sessions of 5-25 min = good ad breaks, but slow hit-rate
D 4  Built-in solver needed if we generate puzzles; trivial if pre-loaded

#2  TAPA
----------------------------------------------------------------
Category:  Nikoli logic puzzle  (gap: "shaded region around numbered cells")
Scores:     P 4  B 5  U 5  M 3  D 4   ->  21 / 25
Slug:       tapa

Why it wins:
  * Same Switch-validated market as Nurikabe - "Puzzle by Nikoli S
    Tapa" exists as a standalone Nintendo release.
  * Ruleset is a near-perfect fit for canvas. Numbered cells give
    "run lengths" of adjacent shaded cells. Validate with a 4-tuple
    pattern check.
  * We already have hitori, akari, slitherlink in the catalog, so
    puzzle-literate users have a natural in-site ladder to Tapa.
  * Visually distinctive: a black/white grid with shaded cells
    forming one connected "blob" - looks great in screenshots and
    social shares.

P 4  Standalone Switch release, large book market
B 5  Grid + click; pattern match is short JS
U 5  Zero catalog overlap
M 3  Same engagement profile as Nurikabe
D 4  Same - solver / pre-loaded set

----------------------------------------------------------------
#3  SUGAR, SUGAR
----------------------------------------------------------------
Category:  Physics drawing  (gap: "draw lines to redirect falling particles")
Scores:     P 5  B 4  U 5  M 4  D 3   ->  21 / 25
Slug:       sugar-sugar

Why it wins:
  * One of the highest-traffic physics-drawing games on the open
    web. Sugar, Sugar 2 is on Poki with ~15,900 user ratings at 4.3
    stars, and is featured on CoolMathGames. Original Sugar, Sugar
    has been on CoolMath since ~2010 - a decade of continuous
    demand.
  * Mechanically distinct from anything in the catalog. We have
    physics-draw-puzzle, brain-it-on, happy-glass, gravity-drop,
    draw-to-home, cover-orange, pin-master, pull-the-pin, etc. -
    every one of those is "draw a one-shot line, release, react to
    the result." Sugar, Sugar is real-time: the user draws while
    particles fall, with continuous interaction.
  * Strong ad economics. Levels last 30-90 s; loop = repeat plays.
  * Buildable in < 30 KB. The original is famously small - under
    20 KB of ActionScript. Canvas port is even smaller; gravity is
    a 5-line loop, line-vs-line intersection is straightforward.

P 5  Top 1% of browser physics-draw games
B 4  Needs requestAnimationFrame particle loop, but no physics engine
U 5  Zero catalog overlap
M 4  30-90 s levels, high loop, strong rewarded-video hooks
D 3  Particle + line geometry is more code than Sudoku; fits but tighter

----------------------------------------------------------------
#4  YAJILIN
----------------------------------------------------------------
Category:  Nikoli logic puzzle  (gap: "single closed loop + arrow cells")
Scores:     P 4  B 5  U 5  M 3  D 3   ->  20 / 25
Slug:       yajilin

Why it wins:
  * Third pillar of the Nikoli Switch trio ("Puzzle by Nikoli W
    Yajilin" - confirmed on Nintendo eShop, also Steam).
  * Most visually striking of the Nikoli family. Players draw a
    single closed loop around a grid, and place black "block" cells
    in response to arrow-number clues. Feels very different from
    anything in the catalog at first sight.
  * Satisfying win moment - the final loop animation is a great
    screenshot/share asset.
  * Slightly higher dev cost than Nurikabe/Tapa because of the
    loop-drawing input and the dual cell-state (white/black), but
    still very tractable in < 40 KB.

P 4  Switch release + Nikoli canon
B 5  Grid + cell states + 1 continuous polyline; small footprint
U 5  Zero catalog overlap
M 3  Same Nikoli engagement profile
D 3  Solver is non-trivial (path + arrow constraints); pre-loaded set recommended

----------------------------------------------------------------
#5  SUGURU  (a.k.a. TECTONIC)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Category:  Number puzzle NOT yet covered  (gap: "irregular sub-region number fill")
Scores:     P 3  B 5  U 5  M 3  D 4   ->  20 / 25
Slug:       suguru

Why it wins:
  * The only number-puzzle gap that is NOT just a sudoku reskin.
    Suguru divides the grid into irregular 2-to-7-cell regions, and
    each region must contain distinct numbers with no row/column
    constraint. It is to sudoku what fillomino is to kakuro.
  * Existing sudoku/killer-sudoku/kenken/futoshiki players will
    pick it up in one read of the rules, but the puzzle is genuinely
    different - easier entry, shorter solves, better for casual
    sessions.
  * Implementation is essentially the same code shape as sudoku.html
    already in /fun/ - we can re-use grid, pencil-mark, hint, and
    timer code. Only the constraint set and the puzzle generator
    change. Estimated < 20 KB.
  * Acts as a feeder for our sudoku-ladder audience without
    cannibalising it.

P 3  Featured in many puzzle books, NYT-published, growing fan base
B 5  Same shape as existing sudoku.html
U 5  Zero catalog overlap
M 3  Faster solves (3-10 min) -> higher loop rate
D 4  Irregular region generator is the only new piece of code

================================================================
KEY UNCERTAINTIES
================================================================
  * Suguru popularity is "real but smaller" than the Nikoli trio.
    Its 20/25 vs. 21/21 gap is honest - it is in the top 5 by score
    tie-break, not by a clear lead. If the next phase wants to be
    conservative, swap to Slant (also 20) for an even simpler build.
  * Sugar, Sugar's dev-complexity 3 reflects the particle loop, not
    the line geometry. 60 KB is achievable but the implementation
    needs a half-day of tuning; treat it as the most "engineering-
    heavy" pick in the top 5.
  * Puzzle generation is the recurring cost driver for the Nikoli
    trio. Mitigation: ship a pre-curated set of 50-80 puzzles per
    game inlined as a string array; total budget stays under 10 KB
    per game. A future "infinite" version can add a 4 KB solver
    later without breaking the 60 KB limit.
